# Website LLC — Y.O.U. (You're Own Vision)

## What This Repository Is
This repository is the operating blueprint for a research-first website studio focused on small businesses that either have no website or have a weak, outdated, low-trust web presence.

The business model is simple:

1. Identify real businesses that need a stronger digital presence.
2. Qualify whether they are a good fit.
3. Build a structured research pack for each business.
4. Turn that pack into a website concept and sales-ready proposal.
5. Secure owner approval before any live publication.

This repo is not just a mission statement anymore. It is the base system for how opportunities should be researched, qualified, documented, and converted into real website projects.

---

## Core Mission
Help overlooked small businesses build a credible online presence quickly, affordably, and responsibly.

We turn scattered public business information into a professional website draft, then work with the owner to approve, refine, and launch a site they can fully own and use to grow.

## Vision
Make quality web presence accessible to local businesses that do great work offline but are difficult to find, trust, or understand online.

## Positioning
This is a research-first website studio for small businesses.

The differentiation is not just website design. The differentiation is the upfront qualification, organization, and business-specific preparation that reduces friction for the owner and makes the sales process easier.

---

## What the Business Actually Does
This business does **not** simply “make websites.”

It:

- identifies small businesses with weak or missing web presence
- researches public-facing business facts
- checks whether the business already has a functional website
- organizes findings into a clean, reusable website brief
- prepares a tailored business improvement plan
- prepares an outreach-ready value proposition
- builds a draft website structure that can be shown to the owner
- secures owner approval before publishing anything live

---

## Qualification Standard
A business is a good fit if most of the following are true:

- no dedicated website exists, or the current website is outdated or low quality
- the business appears active and legitimate
- public information exists to build a strong starter draft
- the business would benefit from stronger credibility, discoverability, or online conversion
- the niche is understandable enough to build a clean first-pass concept quickly

A business is a weak fit if any of the following are true:

- it already has a polished, modern, well-functioning website
- the business appears inactive or permanently closed
- there is not enough public information to build a useful starter pack
- the site opportunity is too large or complex for a lean local-first service offer

## Opportunity Pipeline Workflow

### Phase 1 — Identify
Collect candidate businesses from photos, street research, maps, referrals, or local observation.

### Phase 2 — Qualify
For each business, confirm:

- name
- location
- hours
- phone/contact info if public
- whether a website already exists
- whether their digital presence appears weak enough to improve

### Phase 3 — Filter
Remove businesses that already have a strong website unless there is a clear redesign opportunity.

### Phase 4 — Build Business Pack
Create a structured documentation pack for each qualified business.

### Phase 5 — Create Website Direction
Turn the research into:

- sitemap
- homepage direction
- value proposition
- trust signals
- lead or booking flow
- content recommendations

### Phase 6 — Outreach
Use the pack to support a cold call, email, live pitch, or example preview.

## Standard Deliverable for Each Business
Each business should eventually have its own folder or repo containing the same structured sections.

### 1. Business Profile
- business name
- category
- location
- hours
- contact info
- observed strengths
- observed weaknesses in online presence

### 2. Website Opportunity Brief
- why this business needs a site
- customer trust gaps
- discoverability gaps
- likely conversion opportunities

### 3. Competitive / Digital Presence Snapshot
- whether they have a website
- whether they use Facebook, Instagram, Google Business Profile, Yelp, etc.
- quality of their current digital presence
- what appears missing

### 4. Recommended Website Structure
- homepage
- about
- menu/services
- gallery
- contact/location/hours
- FAQ
- booking/inquiry/order page if relevant

### 5. Content & Asset Notes
- what content can be sourced from public facts
- what must be confirmed by owner
- which assets require permission or replacement

### 6. Action Plan
- shortest path to a presentable draft
- what to build first
- what to confirm before launch
- what upgrades can come later

### 7. Outreach Pitch
- concise explanation of why the business is a fit
- pain point being solved
- why the process is easy for the owner
- next step or call to action

## Ethical Guardrails
This business only works long term if it is built on trust.

Rules:

- public facts may inform early drafts
- final publication requires owner approval
- copyrighted photos, menus, logos, and brand assets cannot be used without permission or license
- claims on the final site must be accurate
- client should own the domain, hosting, and key accounts whenever possible
- no fake testimonials, fake claims, or deceptive impersonation

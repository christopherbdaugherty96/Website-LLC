# Website Care Plans

Status: operating policy

Website care plans are optional monthly services offered after a website build or refresh. They are separate from the one-time website build price.

## Default Ownership Rule

By default, the client should own their domain, hosting, payment accounts, and core business accounts.

Auralis may help set up, configure, or guide these accounts, but ownership should stay clear unless a managed arrangement is explicitly agreed to.

## What Care Plans Are For

Care plans are for ongoing website support after launch, such as:

- small content updates
- basic website checks
- form/link checks
- hosting/domain reminders
- light performance review
- minor copy or image swaps
- basic monthly reporting

## What Care Plans Are Not For

Care plans are not unlimited redesign or new-build work.

The following should usually be quoted separately:

- new pages
- major redesigns
- advanced integrations
- e-commerce setup
- booking systems
- custom automation
- large copy rewrites
- brand redesign
- SEO campaigns beyond basic checks

## Example Care Plan Tiers

### Basic Care

For clients who mainly need peace of mind and occasional small updates.

Possible inclusions:

- monthly website check
- contact form test
- broken link spot check
- one small update per month
- hosting/domain reminder support

### Standard Care

For clients who expect regular updates or want more active support.

Possible inclusions:

- everything in Basic Care
- several small updates per month
- monthly performance/visibility notes
- simple content refreshes
- priority response window

### Managed Care

For clients who want Auralis to stay more involved in their web presence.

Possible inclusions:

- everything in Standard Care
- deeper monthly review
- landing page or campaign support
- more hands-on coordination
- additional reporting

Managed Care should have clearer boundaries because it can easily turn into open-ended work.

## Small Update Definition

A small update may include:

- changing text
- swapping an image
- updating hours
- adding a testimonial
- updating a phone number or email
- minor service wording change

A small update usually should not include:

- new page design
- new feature
- new integration
- major layout change
- full copywriting project

## Cancellation and Handoff

If a client cancels a care plan:

- clarify what support stops
- confirm who owns domain/hosting/accounts
- provide reasonable handoff notes if needed
- do not hold client-owned assets hostage

## Internal Rule

Keep these separate:

- website build
- hosting
- domain ownership
- post-launch updates
- managed care
- new quoted work

Do not blur them in quotes or client conversations.

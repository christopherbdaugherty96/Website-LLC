# Outreach Scripts

Last updated: 2026-04-30

## Core Rule

Specific beats generic. Short beats long. Observation beats pitch.
Always reference what you noticed about their actual business before asking for anything.

---

## The Base Message (adapt per niche)

> Hey [Name] — I looked up [Business Name] and noticed [specific observation].
> I build clean websites for local businesses that make it easy for customers to call, book, or request a quote.
> I can do a free quick review of your current site if that's useful.
> — Christopher, Auralis Digital

Keep it under 4 sentences. Do not explain what a website is. Do not pitch features.

## Niche Scripts (copy, fill in brackets, send)

### Barbershop — No Website

> Hey — I looked up [Shop Name] and noticed you don't have a website yet. A lot of customers search Google before deciding where to go for a cut. I build simple sites for barbershops that show your services, location, and hours so people book instead of guessing. Happy to put together a free concept if you want to see what it could look like.

### Barbershop — Weak Website

> Hey — I checked out [Shop Name]'s site and noticed it's hard to see your services and hours quickly on mobile. A lot of customers decide where to go based on the first site they check. I build clean, fast sites for barbershops that make it easy to call or book. Happy to do a free review.

### Salon — No Website

> Hey — I came across [Salon Name] and noticed there's no website to check out. Customers searching for salons nearby often skip listings without a site. I build simple booking-focused websites for salons. I can put together a free example concept if you'd like to see what it could look like.

### Salon — Weak Website

> Hey — I looked at [Salon Name]'s website and noticed it's hard to browse services and pricing on a phone. Most customers check on mobile before they book. I build clean sites for salons that make that easy. Happy to do a free review.

### Restaurant / Cafe — Weak Presence

> Hey — I found [Restaurant Name] online and noticed it's hard to see the menu and hours quickly. Customers decide where to eat based on that first impression. I build simple sites for restaurants and cafes that show what you offer and how to get there. Happy to do a quick free review.

> Hey — I noticed [Business Name] doesn't have a website. A lot of people searching for cleaning services in [City] filter by whether the company has a site they can trust. I build simple, credible sites for cleaning businesses. Happy to put a free concept together if you want to see it.

### Contractor / Home Service — Weak Presence

> Hey — I found [Business Name] online and noticed the website doesn't make it easy for customers to see what you do and request a quote. I build straightforward sites for local contractors built around getting more calls and estimates. Happy to do a free quick review.

## Where to Send Messages

Priority order:
1. Facebook business page (DM or comment on most recent post)
2. Google Business Profile (if they have a contact option)
3. Phone call (if number is listed)
4. Email (if listed on any directory)

---

## What Not to Do

- Do not send the same generic paragraph to every business
- Do not lead with pricing
- Do not explain what a website is
- Do not use words like "leverage," "digital footprint," or "online ecosystem"
- Do not send more than 2 follow-ups

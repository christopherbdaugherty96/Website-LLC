# Offer, Pricing, and Positioning

## Simple Offer Structure

Your first offer should be easy to understand:

"I build clean, mobile-friendly websites for local businesses that do not already have a strong website presence. I handle the structure, design, and setup so the owner can approve quickly and get online fast."

## Recommended Packages

## Starter Site

Best for: solo operators and very small businesses.

Includes:

- 1 to 3 pages,
- mobile-friendly design,
- contact form,
- Google Maps embed,
- basic on-page SEO setup,
- launch support.

Suggested price range:

- $750 to $1,500

## Growth Site

Best for: established local businesses with several services.

Includes:

- 4 to 7 pages,
- stronger service-page structure,
- FAQ section,
- testimonials section,
- gallery,
- SEO basics,
- analytics and search console setup.

Suggested price range:

- $1,500 to $3,000

## Premium Site

Best for: businesses that need stronger credibility and lead capture.

Includes:

- 7 to 12 pages,
- custom copy assistance,
- booking or inquiry funnel,
- local SEO structure,
- advanced gallery or portfolio,
- more revision time,
- launch training.

Suggested price range:

- $3,000 to $6,000+

## Monthly Maintenance

Includes:

- hosting coordination,
- plugin or dependency updates,
- monthly backup checks,
- simple text and image changes,
- uptime or form checks,
- monthly reporting.

Suggested price range:

- $79 to $299 per month

## Optional Add-Ons

- logo refresh,
- copywriting,
- local SEO cleanup,
- Google Business Profile optimization,
- blog setup,
- booking integration,
- email marketing setup,
- domain and email setup,
- professional photos through a partner.

## Best First Niche Positioning

Do not try to be everything to everyone on day one.

Better:

- "Websites for barbershops and salons"
- "Websites for cleaning companies"
- "Websites for landscapers"
- "Websites for local restaurants"

Why niche focus helps:

- easier outreach,
- easier samples,
- repeatable copy structure,
- faster delivery,
- clearer referrals.

## Your Best Value Proposition

You are not selling "a website."

You are selling:

- credibility,
- easier discovery,
- easier contact,
- better first impressions,
- fewer lost leads,
- less owner effort.

## Pricing Rules

- always charge a deposit,
- never start full production without a signed agreement,
- limit revision rounds,
- price rush work separately,
- price extra pages separately,
- separate build fees from recurring support fees.

## Good Early Payment Structure

- 50% deposit to start,
- 50% before launch or at final approval.

Alternative:

- 40% deposit,
- 40% on design approval,
- 20% before launch.

## What to Avoid Early

- unlimited revisions,
- custom app features,
- complex e-commerce,
- promising SEO rankings,
- underpricing to win every deal,
- bundling too much support into the base fee.

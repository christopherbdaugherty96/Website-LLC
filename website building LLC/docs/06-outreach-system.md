# Outreach System

## Goal

Build a repeatable system that turns public business research into conversations, then into signed website projects.

## Lead Qualification Checklist

Only target businesses that match most of these:

- no real standalone website, or very outdated one,
- active on Google or social,
- clearly local,
- obvious service offering,
- reachable owner or manager,
- contact details publicly listed,
- enough visible business information to understand the basics.

## Best Outreach Workflow

1. Find the business.
2. Verify the website gap.
3. Capture basic facts.
4. Note missing trust signals.
5. Create a short custom observation.
6. Reach out by email, phone, or social DM.
7. Follow up.
8. Book a call.
9. Send proposal and agreement.

## What to Research Before Contacting

- business name,
- location,
- phone number,
- hours,
- service list,
- review themes,
- whether they rely only on Facebook, Instagram, or Google,
- whether they have online booking,
- whether they have obvious missing info customers need.

## The Hook

Do not lead with "I sell websites."

Lead with the business problem:

- customers cannot easily find service details,
- there is no central place for contact info,
- the business looks harder to trust than competitors,
- social profiles are doing too much work.

## Recommended Outreach Channels

Primary:

- email,
- manual phone calls,
- direct messages to business social accounts.

Secondary:

- local networking groups,
- referral partners,
- chambers of commerce,
- photographers, marketers, and sign shops.

## Sample Outreach Strategy

Day 1:

- send email,
- connect with business social account if appropriate.

Day 3:

- send a follow-up email with one concrete observation.

Day 5:

- make a manual phone call.

Day 8:

- send final short follow-up.

Then:

- mark closed or recycle later.

## How to Talk About Your Draft Process

Use language like this:

"I noticed your business is active online, but there is not a strong standalone website yet. I put together a simple concept based on publicly listed business information to show what your site could look like. If you're interested, I can walk you through it and turn it into a finished site with your approval."

This is better than sounding like you already acted as their developer.

## Keep Records

Track:

- business name,
- owner or manager,
- email,
- phone,
- source of lead,
- niche,
- first contact date,
- follow-up dates,
- status,
- notes,
- objections,
- proposal amount,
- close or loss reason.

## First 50-Lead Plan

- choose one niche,
- choose one city or county,
- build a 50-lead sheet,
- contact 10 per week,
- revise messaging after every 10,
- note the objections you hear most,
- adjust offer only after real conversations, not guesses.

## Best Early Offer Close

Instead of trying to close everything on the first message:

- offer a 10 to 15 minute walkthrough,
- show how the site would help,
- explain the price simply,
- ask for a small deposit to begin the approved version.

## Red Flags in Prospects

- asks for unlimited work for cheap,
- wants you to copy another brand directly,
- refuses to sign anything,
- wants access to your personal accounts instead of client-owned accounts,
- cannot confirm rights to their own assets,
- repeatedly changes scope before paying.

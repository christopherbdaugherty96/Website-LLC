# Launch Checklist

Use this before taking your first paying client.

## Legal Setup

- Choose your state of formation.
- Choose your business name.
- Check name availability with your state.
- Decide between sole proprietorship and LLC.
- Register the business if required in your state.
- File any DBA or assumed-name registration if you will operate under a brand name.
- Get an EIN from the IRS if needed or helpful for banking and tax separation.
- Check state and local business license requirements.
- Set up a registered agent if your entity type requires one.

## Financial Setup

- Open a business bank account.
- Set up bookkeeping software or a spreadsheet.
- Create a chart of accounts with revenue, software, hosting, contractor, and marketing categories.
- Decide how you will collect payments.
- Decide deposit terms, milestone terms, and late-fee terms.
- Calendar quarterly estimated tax dates if applicable.

## Contracts and Client Protection

- Customize your service agreement.
- Customize your proposal template.
- Customize your content authorization form.
- Decide your revision limit.
- Decide how change requests will be billed.
- Decide whether hosting is under your account or the client's account.
- Decide how intellectual property transfers after full payment.

## Delivery Setup

- Choose your default website stack.
- Create your reusable client website template.
- Create a launch QA checklist.
- Create a file handoff checklist.
- Create a backup and restore process.
- Create an accessibility baseline checklist.

## Sales Setup

- Choose one niche.
- Choose one city or service area.
- Build a lead tracking sheet or CRM.
- Create a lead qualification checklist.
- Create your cold email sequence.
- Create your cold call opener.
- Create your discovery questionnaire.
- Create your simple proposal deck or PDF.

## Marketing Setup

- Customize your own agency website.
- Add a contact form and business email.
- Add at least two sample projects or concept demos.
- Prepare a short one-sentence explanation of your service.
- Prepare a 30-second and 2-minute pitch.

## Operations Setup

- Buy your domain.
- Set up your business email on your domain.
- Enable two-factor authentication everywhere.
- Store client credentials in a password manager.
- Set up cloud storage and folder naming rules.
- Create standard folders for contracts, content, source files, approvals, and invoices.

## Insurance and Risk Controls

- Get quotes for general liability insurance.
- Get quotes for professional liability or errors-and-omissions insurance.
- Consider cyber insurance if you will manage hosting, forms, or customer data.
- Create a written policy to avoid publishing without owner approval.
- Create a written rule that client logos, photos, and testimonials require permission or written confirmation of rights.

## Green Light to Launch

You are ready when all of these are true:

- you can legally operate in your state,
- you have a bank account and payment process,
- you have a signed-agreement workflow,
- you have a reusable site template,
- you have outreach assets ready,
- you can explain your offer in one sentence,
- you have a clear niche and first lead list.

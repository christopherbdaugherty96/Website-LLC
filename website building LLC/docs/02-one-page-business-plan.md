# One-Page Business Plan

## Business Summary

Business type: Solo website services business for small businesses without a strong web presence.

Primary offer: Fast-turn brochure websites with optional ongoing maintenance.

Primary customer: Local service businesses that rely on calls, walk-ins, referrals, or social media but do not have a modern website.

## Problem

Many small businesses still rely on Facebook pages, Google listings, or word of mouth. That makes them look less credible, harder to find, and harder to contact.

## Solution

You create a professional website draft using public business facts as research input, then convert that draft into a paid project after the owner approves the work, supplies or approves assets, and signs your agreement.

## Why This Model Can Work

- Owners are busy and often delay websites.
- A draft lowers the effort required to say yes.
- Simple brochure sites are faster to sell and deliver than custom software.
- Ongoing maintenance creates recurring revenue.

## Best First Niche Options

Choose one niche first:

- barbershops and salons,
- cleaning services,
- landscapers,
- auto detailers,
- restaurants without a standalone site,
- home repair businesses,
- day spas,
- local gyms or studios,
- pet groomers,
- specialty contractors.

## Ideal Client Profile

- 1 to 20 employees
- active Google Business Profile or social page
- no real website, or an outdated one
- phone-first sales process
- clear local service area
- owner reachable by phone, email, or social DM

## Revenue Model

One-time build:

- Starter site: 1 to 5 pages
- Standard site: 5 to 8 pages
- Premium site: 8+ pages with booking, galleries, FAQs, and stronger SEO setup

Recurring revenue:

- hosting coordination,
- maintenance,
- content updates,
- local SEO cleanup,
- monthly analytics reports,
- reputation or listing support.

## Sales Model

- build a lead list,
- verify no real website exists,
- research public facts,
- create a light mockup or sample structure,
- send outreach,
- book a call,
- close with proposal and deposit,
- launch,
- upsell maintenance.

## Competitive Advantage

You remove friction. Instead of asking an owner to imagine a website, you show them one.

## Main Risks

- copying assets you do not have rights to,
- unclear scope,
- underpricing,
- publishing without written approval,
- not setting up client-owned accounts,
- poor follow-up on leads.

## First-Year Goal

Land 10 to 20 build clients and convert at least 30% of them to a monthly maintenance plan.

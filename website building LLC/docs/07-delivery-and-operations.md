# Delivery and Operations

## Your Delivery Model

Keep it simple and repeatable.

1. Discovery
2. Proposal and deposit
3. Content and asset collection
4. Build
5. Review and revisions
6. Approval
7. Launch
8. Maintenance or handoff

## Discovery Call Goals

- confirm what the business does,
- confirm target customer,
- confirm services and locations,
- confirm what information must be on the site,
- confirm whether the client has assets,
- confirm timeline,
- confirm budget.

## File and Approval Workflow

For every client, keep:

- signed proposal,
- signed agreement,
- invoice and payment record,
- content questionnaire,
- asset folder,
- approval emails or signed approval,
- launch checklist,
- handoff notes.

## Suggested Client Folder Structure

- `01-contracts`
- `02-content`
- `03-assets`
- `04-build`
- `05-approvals`
- `06-launch`
- `07-handoff`

## Minimum Build Standards

- mobile responsive,
- fast-loading basics,
- clear calls to action,
- semantic headings,
- accessible form labels,
- alt text for meaningful images,
- HTTPS,
- business contact info visible,
- analytics if included in scope.

## Launch Checklist

- domain connected,
- SSL active,
- forms tested,
- phone numbers clickable,
- map links correct,
- hours correct,
- spelling reviewed,
- page titles and meta descriptions set,
- favicon added,
- analytics verified,
- backup confirmed,
- final payment received,
- written approval received.

## Best Hosting Rule

Whenever practical, launch into accounts the client owns.

If you temporarily host or register something for them, your agreement should clearly explain:

- who owns it,
- who pays for renewals,
- how transfer works,
- what happens if maintenance ends.

## Maintenance Plan Structure

Offer one simple plan at first:

- monthly plugin or dependency updates if applicable,
- monthly backups,
- uptime or form check,
- 30 to 60 minutes of content edits,
- priority email support,
- quarterly recommendations.

## Scope Control Rules

Use these rules every time:

- each package includes a page limit,
- each package includes a revision limit,
- new features are priced separately,
- rushed deadlines cost extra,
- copywriting beyond light editing costs extra,
- migrations and domain recovery are separate line items.

## How to Handle Public-Info Drafts Safely

When creating a sales draft before a contract:

- keep it private,
- use original structure and wording,
- use placeholder or licensed imagery,
- clearly mark it as a concept,
- do not launch it,
- do not present it as an authorized official site.

## Handoff Checklist

- transfer site files or repository if promised,
- transfer admin access,
- share vendor list,
- share logins through a password manager or secure process,
- confirm who handles renewals,
- confirm whether maintenance continues,
- archive approvals and invoices.

# Small Business Website Agency Starter Package

This package is a practical starting point for launching a U.S.-based business that helps small businesses get online fast.

Your core model in this package is:

1. Find small businesses that do not have a real website.
2. Research their public business facts from listings and social profiles.
3. Create an original website draft using your own design, layout, and copy structure.
4. Contact the owner and offer the draft as a starting point.
5. Only publish after the owner signs your agreement, approves the content, and confirms they have rights to the assets used.

That model matters. Public facts like a business name, address, phone number, hours, and service categories are generally safer to use than copying someone else's photos, graphics, reviews, or written marketing copy.

## Important Notes

- This package assumes you are starting as a solo U.S. founder.
- State and local registration, tax, licensing, and telemarketing rules vary.
- The legal guide is operational guidance, not legal advice.
- Before you accept paying clients, have a local business attorney review your service agreement and approval workflow.
- Before you publish any site built from public information, get written client approval and asset rights confirmation.

## Recommended First Moves

1. Choose your business name, state, and entity structure.
2. Customize the agreement, proposal, and invoice with your real business details.
3. Customize the agency website with your name, pricing, and contact information.
4. Build two sample sites in your niche so your outreach has proof.
5. Start with one niche and one geography for your first 50 leads.

Built the right way

The business model works best when trust is part of the product.

Drafts can be informed by public business information, but final sites should only go live after the owner approves the content and confirms rights to logos, photos, and testimonials.

  • Use public facts as research, not copied content.
  • Do not publish without approval.
  • Launch into client-owned accounts whenever possible.
  • Keep outreach honest and easy to opt out of.

FAQ

Common questions from busy owners.

How fast can a small business site launch?

A simple brochure website can often launch within 1-3 weeks once the client signs, pays the deposit, and approves the content.

Do I need the business owner to provide everything?

No. The model is designed to reduce the burden on the owner. We do the structure and drafting work first, then guide final approvals.

Who owns the finished website?

The goal is for the client to own the final domain, hosting, and approved site assets after full payment, with the exact terms spelled out in the agreement.
